# Draw in the Air — Photography Brief
*Canonical. If a frame breaks the core composition, it does not ship.*

The image must show the real product behaviour clearly.

- The child must always face the laptop, tablet, monitor, or classroom screen.
- The device camera must face the child.
- The child's hand must be raised in front of the screen — drawing, tracing, pinching, dragging, or selecting in the air.
- The screen must show a visible Draw in the Air activity responding to the child's movement.

**Core composition:** Child facing screen. Camera facing child. Hand movement between child and screen. Screen showing active gameplay.

**It fails if:**
- the child looks away from the device,
- the device is not facing the child,
- the child is only smiling beside a laptop,
- the action looks like random waving.

The image must prove the product in one second.

**Approved gestures:** finger tracing in the air · open-hand drawing motion · pinch gesture selecting an item · hand dragging an object in the air · reaching toward a target on screen.

**Approved expressions:** focused concentration · small smile while playing · visible excitement after success · curious attention · proud completion moment. The child should feel genuinely engaged, not posed.

**Home scenes:** A child seated or standing in front of a laptop on a table. The webcam is clearly aligned with the child. The child looks at the screen and traces or pinches in the air. A parent may appear nearby, watching calmly, but never distracting from the child-screen interaction.

**Classroom scenes:** One child or a small group faces a laptop, Chromebook, or large classroom screen. The teacher stands to the side, guiding. The children's eyes and body direction must point toward the screen. At least one child must be actively controlling the game with a hand gesture.

**Screen content:** simple Draw in the Air gameplay — letter tracing · shape sorting · Free Paint · Bubble Pop · Building Mode · Balloon Math. The game UI should be bright, simple, and visible enough to understand.

**Camera alignment:** physically believable. The laptop / tablet / webcam points directly at the child; the child sits inside the camera's likely field of view.

**Lighting:** soft natural daylight. Warm, clean, believable environment.

**Composition:** leave space for marketing copy, but never sacrifice clarity of the interaction.

The viewer should instantly understand: *"This child is controlling the learning game with their hand through the webcam."*

**Final rule:** Every image sells the same idea. Draw in the Air is not children near screens — it is children actively learning through movement, facing the screen, with the webcam reading their hand gestures.
